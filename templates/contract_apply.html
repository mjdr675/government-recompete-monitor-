The steps

{# Step 1 — Register #}
1
Register on SAM.gov (required before you can bid)
Every federal contractor must be registered in the System for Award Management. Registration is free and takes 1–2 weeks. When you register you'll be issued a UEI (Unique Entity Identifier) and a CAGE code — the two IDs the government uses to identify your business. You can't submit a bid without them.
Start SAM.gov registration →
{# Step 2 — Certifications #}
2
Check if you qualify for a set-aside (optional, but a big edge)
Many contracts are reserved for small businesses with specific certifications. If you qualify, your competition shrinks dramatically. The common ones are 8(a), HUBZone, Women-Owned (WOSB), and Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned (SDVOSB).
8(a) HUBZone WOSB SDVOSB

{# Step 4 — Write and submit #}
4
Write and submit your proposal before the deadline
Once the solicitation is posted, download the Statement of Work and evaluation criteria. Proposal deadlines are usually 30–60 days after posting. Your proposal needs to cover technical approach, past performance, pricing, and management. Submit through SAM.gov or whatever method the contracting office specifies. Awards are typically made within 1–6 months — often just weeks before the current contract ends, which is why getting registered and prepared early matters so much.
